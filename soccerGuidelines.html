The Ten General Rules

Rule 1: The Field Dimensions

The field dimensions in soccer should be rectangular, with a length ranging from 100-110 meters (110-120 yards) and a width ranging from 64-75 meters (70-80 yards).

For more details, you can check FIFA's official field dimensions.

Rule 2: The Number of Players

A standard soccer match is played with two teams, each consisting of 11 players, including one goalkeeper.

For variations and specific rules regarding player substitutions, you can refer to FIFA's rules on player substitutions.

Rule 3: Offside Rule

The offside rule is designed to prevent players from gaining an advantage by being closer to the opponent's goal than the ball and the second-to-last defender.

Learn more about the offside rule from FIFA's offside rule guide.

Rule 4: Fouls and Misconduct

Fouls and misconduct in soccer include various actions such as tripping, pushing, and using excessive force. Penalties and disciplinary actions are imposed accordingly.

Get detailed information about fouls and misconduct in soccer from FIFA's rules on fouls and misconduct.

Rule 5: Penalty Kicks

A penalty kick is awarded to the attacking team if a defending player commits a foul inside their own penalty area. It allows a direct shot on goal from the penalty spot.

Learn more about penalty kicks and their regulations in soccer from FIFA's penalty kick guidelines.

Rule 6: Yellow and Red Cards

Yellow and red cards are used by referees to indicate cautions and send-offs for players who violate the rules. Yellow cards are warnings, while red cards result in ejections.

Read about the use of yellow and red cards in soccer in FIFA's referee decision-making section.

Rule 7: Free Kicks

Free kicks are awarded to a team when the opposing team commits a foul. They allow the attacking team to kick the ball without interference from the opponents.

Get more information about free kicks and their rules in soccer from FIFA's guidelines on free kicks.

Rule 8: Throw-ins

When the ball goes out of play along the sidelines, a throw-in is taken to restart the game. The throw-in is awarded to the team opposite to the one that last touched the ball.

Learn about the correct technique and rules for throw-ins in soccer from FIFA's throw-in guidelines.

Rule 9: Goal Kicks

A goal kick is awarded to the defending team when the ball completely crosses the goal line, but not into the goal, after being last touched by the attacking team.

Discover more about goal kicks and their procedures in soccer from FIFA's rules on goal kicks.

Rule 10: Corner Kicks

Corner kicks are awarded to the attacking team when the ball goes out of play over the goal line, but not into the goal, after being last touched by the defending team.

Find out more about corner kicks and their rules in soccer from FIFA's corner kick guidelines.
